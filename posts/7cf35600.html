英语句子摘抄

Created2022-09-09|Updated2022-09-14|学英语
|Word count:2k|Reading time:10min|Post View:|Comments:
  1. What Gen-Z graduates want from their employers
    1. With graduation ceremonies behind them (指刚刚结束毕业典礼), the latest batch of diploma-holders (拿着学位证的人，作者对批量生产大学生不满的春秋笔法) is entering the job market.
    2. And as the economy sours (恶化，变化) following a pandemic jobs boom, those wants are in flux (不断地变化).
    3. The option to work remotely is increasingly non-negotiable (没有商量的余地).
    4. Industries with jobs that cannot be done from home are falling out of favor (失宠) with recent graduates.
    5. There are signs that Gen-Z’s love affair (热烈的喜爱) with tech may be losing some of its ardour (热爱，热情).
    6. Those graduates who do choose tech are likelier to pick an established firm over a sexy (令人激动的) startup with hazier prospects (看不清楚的前景).
    7. Some graduates may instead opt for(选择) other high-tech sectors that seem less vulnerable (易受攻击的) to economic swings.
    8. Drugmakers at the forefront (前景) of the covid-19-vaccine rollout (新产品推出) are finding particular favour. AstraZeneca (阿斯利康) and Pfizer (辉瑞), each of which has produced an effective jab, shot up(指排名上升得像子弹发射一样快) in the rankings of Britain’s most attractive employers last year.
    9. The best thing firms an do to attract young talent is to cough up (cough的意思是咳嗽，这里指很勉强艰难的憋出来钱) more money.
    10. Some earlier Gen-Z hobby horses (被过多谈论的话题) such as an employer’s commitment to diversity and inclusion or corporate social responsibility have edged down (数字或价格的下降，也有edge up 表示上涨) the list of American graduates’ priorities.

  2. Modern biology has produced a genuinely new way of looking at the world that is incidentally congenial to the inner direction of biophilia. In other words, instinct is in this rare instance aligned with reason (“本能”很偶然地和理性融合起来了). The conclusion I draw is optimistic: To the degree that we come to understand other organisms, we will place a greater value on them, and on ourselves.

  3. Flipped. Wendelin van Draanen

    1. She didn’t just barge into my life. She barged and shoved and wedged her way into my life.

    2. I knew right off that this was a ditch play. And I didn’t think about it until later, but ditch wasn’t a play I’d run with my dad before. Face it, pulling a ditch is not something discussed with dads. It’s like, against parental law to tell your kid it’s okay to ditch someone, no matter how annoying or muddy they might be.

    3. Finally I break free and do the only manly thing available when you’re seven years old — I dive behind my mother.

    4. And after those were off, my mom told her that her dirty socks had to go, too. Juli wasn’t embarrassed. Not a bit. She just peeled them off and left them in a crusty heap on your porch.

    5. My sister, on the other hand, tried to sarbotage me any chance she got.

    6. She’d edged her nose pratically up to my scalp and sniff-sniff-sniff. 她几乎把鼻子贴到我的头皮上然后闻闻闻。这里edge作为动词表示缓慢移动，pratically表示几乎，差不多

    7. And since I couldn’t see her perfectly penned answers anymore, my grades took a dive.

    8. The first day I met Bryce Loski, I flipped. Honestly, one look at him and I became a lunatic. 看到他的第一眼，我就癫狂了。It’s his eyes. Something in his eyes. They’re blue, and framed in the blackness of his lashes, they’re dazzling. Absolutely breathtaking.

    9. I thought I would die for wanting to be with him.

    10. Oh, there were kids, but every one of them was older. That was dandy (极好的) for my brothers, but what it left me was home alone.

    11. Mostly he’d just block because a dog can’t exactly kick and score, but once in a while he’d dribble (运球) with his nose.

    12. My heart stopped. It just stopped beating. And for the first time in my life, I had that feeling. You know, like the world is moving all around you, all beneath you, all inside you, and you’re floating. Floating in the midair. And the only thing keeping you from drifting away is the other person’s eyes. They’re connected to yours by some invisible physical force, and they hold you fast while the rest of the world swirls and twirls and falls completely away. 有一说一，这段读起来真舒服。英语里也有排比句呢。

    13. My mouth was watering just looking at it! 这句话是说Juli看到妈妈把自己做的Lemon Bundt cake送给Bryce的妈妈是馋得流口水。

    14. Then in the fifth grade Shelly Stalls came into the picture.

    15. Now that we’re in junior high, she’s the undisputed (无可争议的) diva of drama, but even back in elementary school she knew how to put on a performance. 意思是说Shelly Stalls特别能装，人前一套背后一套

    16. What bugged me about it was not the fact that she got out of P.E. — Who’d want her on their team, anyway? What bugged me about it was that it wasn’t asthma or weak ankles or her being “delicate” that was stopping her. It was her hair. She had mountains of it, twisted this way or that, clipped or beaded, braided or swirled. 这里bug作动词表示“使厌烦”，Shelly让我讨厌的不是她不上体育课，毕竟有谁想和她在一个小队里呢？让我讨厌的是，她逃课并不是因为哮喘或者身体不好，二是因为她的头发，她有山一样多的头发，这样或那样扭曲(twisted)，剪裁(clipped)或串珠(beaded)，编织(braided)或旋转(swirled)。

    17. How could Shelly have wormed (塞入) her hand into his? That pushy (咄咄逼人的) little princess had no business hanging on to him like that! Shelly怎么能把手伸进Bryce的手里呢？那个咄咄逼人的小公主没有权利像这样粘着他 (hang on to him, 挂在他身上)

    18. I came right out and told her that it was not a magnificent sycamore, it was, in reality, the ugliest tree known to man. And you know what she said? She said I was visually challenged. Visually challenged! This from the girl who lives in a house that’s the scourge (灾难，祸害) of the neighborhood. 这里因为Bryce说Juli的树是人类见过的最丑的树，被Juli骂有视力障碍 (visually challenged)，challenged的意思是某方面有残疾的。

    19. Whatever. Maybe Juli’s aesthetic (美学的) sensibilities have been permanently screwed up (搞砸了) by her father and none of this is her fault, but Juli has always thought that that sycamore tree was God’s gift to our little corner of the universe. 也许Juli的审美被他被她的父亲永远地搞砸了，这不是她的错，但Juli总是相信那棵美国梧桐是上帝放在我们这个宇宙的小小角落的一份礼物。

    20. I was relieved, but I also felt like a weenie. When I’d seen where my kite was trapped, I was sure it was a goner (已死之人). Not Juli. She scrambled up and got it down in no time. Man, it was embarrassing. 这句话是在Bryce的风筝被挂到了美国梧桐树上，Juli毫不费力地爬上去帮他取下来之后。Bryce感到非常不好意思和尴尬。这里Weenie的意思是虚弱的、社交废物、书呆子一类的人。

    21. I trucked up the hill, and as I got closer and saw what the men were holding, I figured out in a hurry what was making Juli refuse to come out of the tree.

      Chain saws. 锯条

      Don’t get me wrong here, okay? The tree was an ugly mutant tangle of gnarly branches. The girl arguing with those men was Juli — the world’s peskiest (让人讨厌的), bossiest (专横的), most know-it-all female. But all of a sudden my stomach completely bailed on me. Juli loved that tree. Stupid as it was, she loved that tree, and cutting it down would be like cutting out her heart. 粗体的句子，我不太理解。

    22. My grandfather’s a big man with a meaty nose and greased-back salt-and-pepper hair. 我的祖父是个大个子，鼻子很肥，头发油腻腻的（有着一头刷着动物油脂的、盐和胡椒粉的头发）。 He lives in house slippers and a sports coat (他穿着居家拖鞋和运动外套), and I’ve never seen a whisker (胡须) on him. They grow, but he shaves them off like three times a day. It’s a real recreational (娱乐的，消遣的) activity for him.

    23. I couldn’t just stand up and leave, even though that’s what I felt like doing. Somehow I knew if I left like that, he’d quit talking to me at all. Even about salt. So I sat there feeling sort of tortured. Was he mad at me? How could he be mad at me? I hadn’t done anything wrong! 祖父跟Bryce的对话让他感到像在被拷问 (tortured) 一样。

    24. Then one day it rained and I thought for sure she’d be up at the bus stop, but no. Garrett said he saw her trucking along on her bike in a bright yellow poncho (雨衣), and in math I noticed that her pants were still soaked from the knees down.

      When math let out, I started to chase after her to tell her that she ought to try riding the bus again, but I stopped myself in the nick of time (在关键时刻). What was I thinking? That Juli wouldn’t take a little friendly concern and completely misinterpret it? Whoa now, buddy, beware! Better to just leave well enough alone.

      After all, the last thing I needed was for Juli Baker to think I missed her. 我最不需要的就是让Juli Baker认为我想念她。
